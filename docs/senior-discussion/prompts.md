# Senior Discussion Prompts

> The 20-ish prompts that show up in senior/staff loops, grouped by theme.

Prepare **one story per theme** minimum. Ideal: 2 stories per theme, one recent + one different-domain.

---

## Theme 1 — Tough technical decision

- "Tell me about a technical decision you made that had significant trade-offs."
- "Describe a time you chose one architecture over another. Why?"
- "When have you rejected a popular technology or pattern?"
- "Tell me about a rewrite decision — rewrite or refactor?"

**What they're testing**: judgment, ability to reason under constraint, willingness to defend a position.

**Key beats**: options considered, criteria, trade-off explicitly named, what you'd do differently.

---

## Theme 2 — Cross-team / influence without authority

- "Tell me about a time you drove alignment across multiple teams."
- "Describe a time another team's decision was going to hurt yours. What did you do?"
- "How did you get a design approved by a skeptical stakeholder?"
- "Tell me about a time you influenced a decision above your level."

**What they're testing**: staff+ signal — you shape outcomes beyond your team.

**Key beats**: who cared, their concerns, how you built the case (data, prototype, 1:1s), the alignment moment, the outcome.

---

## Theme 3 — Ambiguity / unclear scope

- "Tell me about a project where the requirements weren't clear."
- "Describe a time you had to make a decision without complete information."
- "How did you approach a problem no one on the team had seen before?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to reframe a problem."

**What they're testing**: comfort operating without a spec; ability to define the problem, not just solve it.

**Key beats**: initial ambiguity, how you narrowed it (talked to users, prototyped, wrote a doc), the reframing insight, the outcome.

---

## Theme 4 — Disagreement / conflict

- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager."
- "Describe a technical disagreement with a peer. How did you resolve it?"
- "When did you have to push back on a deadline or scope?"
- "Tell me about a code review that turned into a debate."

**What they're testing**: can you disagree without burning bridges; can you change your mind when you should.

**Key beats**: the substance of the disagreement, how you engaged (not avoided), the resolution mechanism (data, experiment, escalation, agree-to-disagree), the relationship afterward.

!!! warning "Avoid these anti-patterns"
    - "I was right and they came around" (arrogance signal)
    - "I gave in to keep peace" (no conviction)
    - Aim for: "We ran the experiment / prototype and let the data decide" or "I updated my view when I learned X"

---

## Theme 5 — Failure / thing that went wrong

- "Tell me about a project that didn't go well."
- "What's the biggest mistake you've made technically?"
- "Describe an incident you owned. What happened?"
- "Tell me about feedback that stung."

**What they're testing**: self-awareness, humility, ability to learn.

**Key beats**: own it clearly, no blame, technical + process root cause, personal lesson, systemic fix.

---

## Theme 6 — Mentoring / multiplier

- "Tell me about someone you mentored."
- "How have you leveled up your team?"
- "Describe a time you delegated something you were tempted to do yourself."
- "Tell me about a code review or design review culture you shaped."

**What they're testing**: leverage — do you make others better, or just execute?

**Key beats**: specific person + specific growth, what you did concretely (not just "gave advice"), the outcome for them, the outcome for the team.

---

## Theme 7 — Scope / prioritization

- "Tell me about a time you cut scope."
- "How did you decide what NOT to build?"
- "Describe a project where you pushed back on requirements."
- "Tell me about a time you deprecated something."

**What they're testing**: engineering leadership — saying no, focusing on impact, killing your darlings.

**Key beats**: what was on the table, the criteria for cutting, who you had to convince, what didn't get built, why the outcome was better.

---

## Theme 8 — Learning / growth

- "How do you stay current?"
- "Tell me about a skill you've deliberately developed in the last year."
- "What's something you've changed your mind about?"
- "How do you approach learning a new codebase / domain?"

**What they're testing**: growth mindset, self-direction, intellectual honesty.

**Key beats**: specific systems (blogs, books, side projects, mentors), a concrete example of applied learning, an explicit "I used to think X, now I think Y".

---

## Questions to ask THEM

Ask 2-3 that signal seniority. Avoid softballs ("what's the culture like?").

**Good**:
- "What's the biggest technical debt the team is carrying, and what's your plan for it?"
- "How do decisions get made when the team disagrees on architecture?"
- "What's a project that failed here in the last year, and what did the org take away from it?"
- "Where do you see this team in 2 years? What has to be true for that to happen?"
- "How do senior engineers here spend their time — more code, more design, more mentorship?"
- "What would make me successful in the first 90 days that isn't in the JD?"

**Skip**:
- "What's the tech stack?" (Google it)
- "Is there work-life balance?" (they'll say yes)
- "What are opportunities for growth?" (too generic)
